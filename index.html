Global Analysis of a Dynamical Model
for Tuberculosis Transmission

with a General Contact Function


Samuel Bowong, Jean Jules Tewa

Potsdam Institute for Climate Impact Research, Germany

Motivation

  • Tuberculosis (TB) is a leading cause of death from a single infectious agent
  • One-third of the world's population is infected with M. tuberculosis
  • 2–3 million deaths annually
  • HIV, MDR-TB, poverty, and crowding accelerate transmission

Contact Function \(\beta(N)\)

  • Mass action: \(\beta(N) = \beta\)
  • Standard incidence: \(\beta(N) = \beta/N\)
  • Saturating: \(\displaystyle \beta(N) = \frac{bN}{1+bN+\sqrt{1+2bN}}\)

Assumptions: \(\beta(N)>0,\ \beta'(N)\le 0,\ (N\beta(N))'\ge 0\)

SEI Model Structure

\[ \begin{cases} \dot S = \Lambda - \beta(N) S I - \mu S,\\[6pt] \dot E = \beta(N)(1-p)SI + r_2 I - [\mu+k(1-r_1)]E,\\[6pt] \dot I = \beta(N)p SI + k(1-r_1)E - (\mu+d+\delta+r_2)I \end{cases} \]

  • \(S\) – Susceptible, \(E\) – Exposed (latent), \(I\) – Infectious
  • \(\Lambda\) – recruitment rate, \(\mu\) – natural death rate
  • \(p\) – fast progression, \(k\) – reactivation rate
  • \(r_1\) – chemoprophylaxis, \(r_2\) – treatment rate
  • \(d\) – disease-induced death, \(\delta\) – emigration rate

Basic Reproduction Number \(\mathcal{R}_0\)

\[ \mathcal{R}_0 = \frac{\beta(S_0)S_0\bigl[\mu p + k(1-r_1)\bigr]}{(\mu+d+\delta)\bigl[\mu+k(1-r_1)\bigr] + \mu r_2},\qquad S_0 = \frac{\Lambda}{\mu} \]


  • \(\mathcal{R}_0\) = average number of secondary infections from one infectious individual in a fully susceptible population
  • \(\mathcal{R}_0 < 1\) → Disease-free equilibrium (DFE) is locally asymptotically stable
  • \(\mathcal{R}_0 > 1\) → DFE unstable, endemic equilibrium appears

Global Stability of Disease-Free Equilibrium

Theorem: If \(\mathcal{R}_0 \le 1\), the DFE \(P_0 = (S_0,0,0)\) is globally asymptotically stable.


Lyapunov function:

\[ V(E,I) = k(1-r_1)E + \bigl[\mu + k(1-r_1)\bigr]I \]

Its time derivative satisfies:

\[ \dot V \le \bigl[r_2\mu + (\mu+k(1-r_1))(\mu+d+\delta)\bigr](\mathcal{R}_0 - 1)I \le 0 \]

By LaSalle's invariance principle, all trajectories converge to \(P_0\).

Endemic Equilibrium

When \(\mathcal{R}_0 > 1\), there exists a unique positive endemic equilibrium \(P^* = (S^*,E^*,I^*)\).


Global Stability of \(P^*\)

Under the conditions:

\[ \frac{S}{S^*} \le \frac{E}{E^*},\qquad \frac{S}{S^*} \le \frac{I}{I^*} \]

the endemic equilibrium is globally asymptotically stable.


Lyapunov function:

\[ U = S - S^*\ln S + A(E - E^*\ln E) + B(I - I^*\ln I) \]

with appropriately chosen constants \(A, B > 0\).

Numerical Simulations

  • Case 1: \(\beta(N)=\beta\) (mass action) → convergence to DFE or endemic equilibrium depending on \(\mathcal{R}_0\)
  • Case 2: \(\beta(N)=\beta/N\) (standard incidence) → same threshold behavior
  • Case 3: Saturating contact function → same qualitative behavior

Conclusion

  • A TB model with a general contact function was analyzed
  • Threshold dynamics determined by \(\mathcal{R}_0\)
  • Global stability proven using Lyapunov functions
  • Numerical simulations confirm theoretical results
